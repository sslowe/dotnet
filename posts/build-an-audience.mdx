The Endeavor continues! So many tasks to go! A vanishing amount of time to do it in! If you'd like to catch up on the most ambitious way I've ever set myself up to fail publicly, [start here](/posts/document-everything). Today, we make it even public-er.

I've always been an online lurker first and foremost, content to consume articles and commentary (or, more realistically, memes and comments) uploaded by others. Social media never really did much for me, either. Outside of using Facebook for events in college and a run at using my Instagram for music promotion, the handful of posts I ever made in my younger years are long since archived. I massively disliked the experience of curating any sort of online persona, and sublimated the wisdom to not post anything I might regret into never posting anything to begin with. How fortuitous, then, that I contrived a situation to force myself into being the most nakedly opinionated I've ever been. I'm sure none of this will age poorly *at all*.

I can pinpoint the exact moment in time it happened, too. The year was... 2008. Yes, I know, another childhood anecdote, but cut me some slack. There's a lot of delayed gratification going on here. Anyway, 2008. My obsession at the time was Neopets, and I was in *deep*. It got to the point that I was getting my parents to wake me up early for school so that I could integrate dailies like spinning the Wheel of Monotony or visiting the shrine in the desert into my morning routine. It was also the one and only time I actively participated in an online community in the form of a Neopets Guild. <span className="text-[#77FFE4] text-sm">[Side note: the notion of daily tasks in browser games is jogging my memory of an awesome but short-lived text-RPG tie-in to Terminator: Salvation that I also played religiously. Every time I've tried to find more information about it has turned up a dead end, I assume because of the volume of games in the franchise and the general ephemerality of promotional tie-ins. This time around, there's a new tabletop RPG by Nightfall games muddying the results even further. If anyone can point me in the right direction, let me know.]</span>

I was one of the more active members of my guild, and developed a friendship with one member in particular, chatting back and forth whenever we were online. In 2008, I was 10, going on 11, and my brother was 18 and about to matriculate at North Carolina State University. For those who did not attend elementary school in North Carolina, a brief synopsis: *Duke is puke. Wake is fake. The team I hate is NC State. You can't go to heaven in a red canoe, 'cause God's favorite color is Carolina blue.* I count among NC State's manifold shortcomings the fact that they sent my parents home from freshman orientation convinced that everyone on the internet was a child predator. Any communications with strangers on the internet was put on indefinite hold, and with it, my time as an active online participant. Digital or not, there was a distinct sense of loss when I was suddenly unable to talk to my Neopets friend, and I just never had the heart to get invested in any sort of web-based community again at the risk of replicating that feeling.

Before I move on, I should close this story with the disclaimer that my ~~mother~~ editor will undoubtedly take umbrage if anyone thinks that she was in any way callous or overly strict. Fear not, ~~mother~~ editor, I know you had my best interests at heart. And no, before anyone asks, my ~~mother~~ editor is not forcing me to say this under threat of desertion. 

I think it's high time to subject the open internet to my thoughts once more. And while I would be more than happy to toil in obscurity on my little corner of the internet that is [sacramo.net](https://sacramo.net), who am I to deny any of you twisted sisters who might be picking up what I'm putting down, so to speak.

## The blogramo is on Substack!

To that end, I've decided to start syndicating the blogramo on Substack. If you'd like to receive new posts directly in your inbox in newsletter form, subscribe at [sacramo.substack.com](https://sacramo.substack.com). If you simply prefer the experience of reading on Substack, I promise not to take it *too* personally.

[sacramo.net](https://sacramo.net) will continue to be the primary home of the blogramo, with posts appearing here a day or two before circulating in newsletter form. As someone who thinks a lot about the internet and platforms, I like the idea of truly *owning* my own words, independent of any third party. This isn't a new idea and is known in IndieWeb circles as POSSE: [Publish (on your) Own Site, Syndicate Elsewhere](https://indieweb.org/POSSE). Also if I'm being persnickety, I think my RSS feed is cleaner than the one generated by Substack.

I went back and forth over whether I also wanted to join a microblogging platform like Mastodon or Bluesky, but POSSE works best when you adhere to the conventions of the respective community, meaning prior experience with the platform counts. Mastodon, in particular, appeals to the technology geek in me, but for now I've settled on doubling down on what I'm already enjoying doing, which is writing these posts. I've got a lot in store this week (I better if I'm going to get anywhere near the end of this list), so stay tuned.
